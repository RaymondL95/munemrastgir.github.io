Descriptions about the assigned body part.

Biceps, triceps, forearm descriptions

1.	Alternating dumbbell curl, biceps
Stand upright with a dumbbell in each hand with the palms of your hand facing your thighs. While keeping the upper limb stationary, curl the dumbbell and turn your palm 90 degrees at the beginning until forearm touches your biceps. Return to starting position and repeat with the other arm.

2.	Alternating hammer curl, biceps
Stand upright with dumbbell in each hand with the palms of your hand facing your thighs. While keeping the upper limb stationary, curl the dumbbell until forearm touches your biceps. Return to starting position and repeat with the other arm.

3.	Barbell curl, biceps
Stand upright while holding a barbell at shoulder width and palms supinated. Curl the barbell until forearms touch your biceps. Return to starting position and repeat. 

4.	Alternating preacher curl, biceps
Get a dumbbell with either arm and place the upper limb on top of a preacher bench or something with an incline. The starting position of this exercise is when the biceps is contracted or when forearm touches your biceps. Slowly lower the dumbbell until arm is fully extended. Return to starting position and repeat with the other arm after finish the reps for one side.

5.	Concentration curls, biceps
Grab a dumbbell with either hand and sit down on a flat bench. Spread your legs with knees bent and place the elbow of the arm with the dumbbell on same side of your inner thigh. Your arm should be fully extended and supinated. Curl the dumbbell until your forearm touches your biceps. Return to starting position and repeat with the other arm after finish the reps for one side.

6.	Dips, triceps
Go to a dip station; get into the starting position with arms fully extended and body at arm’s length. Slowly lower your body downward and keep your knees bent for balance. Your elbows should be close to your body and your torso should be upright. Lower your torso until there is a right angle between your forearm and upper limb. Push yourself back up to the starting position with your triceps. Repeat

7.	EZ-bar skullcrusher, triceps
Lie down on a flat bench with your arms pronated in a closer than shoulder width grip and fully extended holding an EZ bar. This will be the starting position, when your arms are perpendicular to the floor. Keeping the upper limbs stationary, lower the bar and stop right before crushing the skull. Lift the bar back to starting position by extending the elbow. Repeat.

8.	Dumbbell wrist curl, forearms
Grab a dumbbell with each of your hands in a pronated grip and place your forearms flat on a flat bench with your wrists on the edge. Your body will be in kneeling position. Curl the dumbbell upward and slowly lower your wrist back to starting position. Repeat. 

9.	Triceps Rope Pushdown, triceps
Attach a rope attachment to a high pulley and hold on to each ends of the rope with a neutral grip. Stand close to the pulley with a small bent forward, bring the upper limbs close to your body and perpendicular to the floor. This will be the starting position. While keeping the elbows stationary, push down the rope with your triceps until your arms are fully extended and perpendicular to the floor. Slowly return the rope back to starting position and repeat. 

10.	Close grip bench press, triceps
Lie down on a flat bench and set up like performing normal bench press but with closer grip. Lift the bar from the rack with your arms fully extended, this will be the starting position. While keeping the elbows close to the torso, slowly lower the bar until it touches the chest. Push the bar back up to the starting position using your triceps. Repeat. 

•	Squat, quadriceps
With a barbell on your upper back, move your legs and glutes downward as if sitting down in a chair and then stand back up. Make sure your knees don’t go past your toes and keep your back straight.

•	Front Squat, quadriceps
Have a barbell resting on your front shoulders and have your arms crossed in front of you to support it. Bend your knees and go downwards until your upper legs and calves form a 90-degree angle and then go up.

•	Deadlift, hamstrings
Have a barbell on the floor in front of you and bend down with your back straight so your hands can grasp the bar. Pull up the bar using your legs and back until you are standing completely straight up. Keep your shoulders back and chest outwards and don’t pull with your arms to ensure balance and proper muscular involvement.

•	Leg Press, quadriceps
Sit down on the leg press machine and have your legs slightly bent on the starting position. Remove the locks and then bend your knees until your legs reach a 90-degree angle. Push up on the weight with your legs until you are back to the starting position.

•	Lunge, quadriceps
Stand up straight with 2 dumbbells in your hands. Take a wide step with one leg while your other leg is almost touching the floor. Then stand up straight and repeat the wide step with your other leg. Keep your back straight and make sure your steps are long to stretch the muscle

•	Calf Raises, calves
Stand up with your legs slightly bent on the calf raise machine with the shoulder pads resting on your shoulders. Lift the heels of your feet up so you are standing on your tiptoes and then put your feet back down.

•	Leg Curl, hamstrings
Lie down on your chest with the movement pad resting on the back of your ankles. Push your feet towards your body to curl your upper leg and then move your feet back down to the starting position.

•	Leg Extension, quadriceps
Sit on the leg curl machine with the movement pad resting on the front of your ankles. Kick your legs up until they are straight and then move them back down.

Bench Press

-	Lie flat on the bench, Lift the bar out of the rack straight above your chest with arms locked. Come down slowly until the bar touches the middle of your chest. After a brief pause push the bar straight up while breathing out.

Incline Bench Press
-	Sit down on the bench with head rested on the seat. Lift the bar out above your chest. With elbows pointed straight slowly come down on your chest. After a brief pause lift the bar back above your chest.

Decline Bench Press
-	Secure your legs at the end of the decline bench and slowly lay down on the bench.  Hold the bar above you, with elbows pointed straight slowly descend until it touches your chest. After a brief pause return to starting position.

Chest Flyes
-	Lie down on a bench with elbows slightly bent. Slowly bring your arms down in an arc until you feel a stretch in your chest. Make sure to breathe in during this portion of the exercise. Afterwards return to starting position.

Dips (chest)
-	Hold your body at arms length above parallel bars. While breathing in lower your body with your chest slightly leaning forward (30 degrees). When you feel a stretch in your chest slowly breathe in and return to the starting position.
